El silencio de la sintaxis: una introducción al fenómeno de la elipsis

(The Silence of Syntax: An introduction to ellipsis)

Laura Stigliano
(The Ohio State University)



This course will be taught in Spanish

Abstract

This course is an introduction to the phenomenon of ellipsis from a syntactic perspective. Ellipsis is a phenomenon in which, under particular circumstances, certain linguistic material can be omitted, but it’s still understood in the context of its antecedent. Ellipsis’ importance lies in the fact that it represents a case in which the association between linguistic form and meaning breaks down: there is meaning without form.

Ellipsis has been a central topic in the study of linguistics because it raises fundamental questions about language in particular, and cognition in general. What are the linguistic and extralinguistic conditions on the licensing of ellipsis? What are the possible and impossible interpretations of the different types of ellipsis and how are they determined? What mechanisms allow us to assign meaning to silence?

In this course, we’ll explore the complexities of this phenomenon, focusing on the different kinds of ellipsis and carrying out a contrastive study of Spanish and English ellipsis, highlighting the crosslinguistic differences.
