Ciclicidad y estratificación: una introducción a la teoría de la optimidad estratal a partir de fenómenos del español y el catalán (Cyclicity and Stratification: An introduction to Stratal Optimality Theory based on Spanish and Catalan phenomena).

Francesc Torres-Tamarit (Centre de Lingüística Teòrica, Universitat Autònoma de Barcelona)



This course will be taught in Spanish

Abstract

The aim of this course is to provide an introduction to Stratal Optimality Theory, a model based on the interaction of universal constraints that incorporates the concepts of cyclicity and stratification. According to this model, phonology operates cyclically over increasingly larger morphosyntactically defined domains: the root, the word, and the utterance. Stratification refers to the fact that these domains are associated with constituents obeying different phonological generalizations, which result in different rankings of constraints. Stratal Optimality Theory will be illustrated with phonological processes in Spanish and Catalan, specifically, rhotic vibrantization, /n/ velarization, and /s/ aspiration in Spanish, including /j/ coronalization in Argentine Spanish, as well as /ʒ/ affrication, final-obstruent devoicing and the interaction between the assimilation of the place of articulation of nasals and the simplification of consonant clusters in Catalan.

Scheme of classes

  • Lecture 1: Introduction to Formal Semantics
  • Lecture 2: The Semantics of Nominal Phrases
  • Lecture 3: Bare Nominals across languages
  • Lecture 4: Plurals and mass
  • Lecture 5: Comparison and the Mass-Count distinction
