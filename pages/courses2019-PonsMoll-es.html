Constraint-based models applied to the phonology of Romance languages

Clàudia Pons-Moll (Universitat de Barcelona)



Resumen (en inglés)

The purpose of this one-week course is to introduce the assistants to the basics of the mainstream constraint-based models of phonological analysis, specifically to Optimality Theory (Prince & Smolensky 1993 / 2004) and to Harmonic Grammar (Smolensky 1986, Smolensky & Legendre 2006). The course will show how these models can shed light on a variety of syllabically-driven phenomena drawn from Romance languages (including European and Latin American Spanish, Catalan, Sardinian, European and Brazilian Portuguese, etc.), and on a set of patterns found in the loanword phonology of some Romance languages. The course will also illustrate how these models deal with phonological variation, phonological change, and phonological acquisition and learning.

Scheme of classes

  • Lecture 1: Optimality Theory (1): The basics
  • Lecture 2: Optimality Theory (2): Applications to Romance phonology
  • Lecture 3: Harmonic Grammar (1): The basics
  • Lecture 4: Harmonic Grammar (2): Applications to loanword phonology
  • Lecture 5: Phonological variation, phonological change, and phonological acquisition and learning
