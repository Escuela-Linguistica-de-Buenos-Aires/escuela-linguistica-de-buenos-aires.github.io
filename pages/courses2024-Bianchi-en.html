Uso de modelos de Procesamiento de Lenguaje Natural para el estudio del lenguaje en el cerebro (Use of Natural Language Processing models for the study of language in the brain)

Bruno Bianchi
(Universidad de Buenos Aires)



This course will be taught in Spanish

Abstract

In recent years, we have witnessed rapid development in the field of NLP, particularly concerning computational models and their ability to understand and generate text in a human-like way. The most prominent example is the ChatGPT system, developed by the company OpenAI. These advances have not been unfamiliar to the Computational Cognitive Neuroscience community, especially to Neurolinguistics, a discipline that stands to benefit greatly from such tools in understanding the mechanisms operating in the brain during language-related tasks. In this course, we will explore some of the techniques used in recent years in this area. In the first half of the course (the first 2 classes out of a total of 4), we will provide an introduction to computational models, offering a historical overview of different abstraction generation techniques (embeddings) to gradually cover current models and their architectures. In the second half of the course, we will delve into discussions on various works in the field of neuroscience, where the abstractions generated by computational models are used as tools for the analysis of neuroimaging data, primarily Functional Magnetic Resonance Imaging (fMRI).
